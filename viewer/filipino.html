Plain-language explanation

Several possible readings—not one forced pronunciation

The experimental pronunciation lattice keeps the published name and creates controlled comparison paths for Filipino nativization, vowel variation, casual speech and stress position. It does not split people into Tagalog and Bisaya groups, and it never changes the official medicine name.

How this differs from Pair Review: Pair Review ranks every high/medium candidate using spelling, phonetic and Filipino-aware signals together. This page is a focused explanation view showing only candidates where at least one Filipino-aware signal contributed. It is a subset, not a second algorithm or a separate dataset.
1

Build reading paths

Keep the published spelling, then add Filipino-nativized, vowel-merged, casual-speech and stress alternatives.

2

Split into syllables

Compare syllable structure as well as individual letters, so longer spoken chunks can align.

3

Align speech features

Weighted phoneme alignment gives smaller penalties to controlled pairs such as f/p, v/b, e/i and o/u.

4

Combine explainable signals

A fixed-weight score combines the lattice, phonemes, syllables, stress and letter bigrams. It is not a trained or validated accuracy score.
