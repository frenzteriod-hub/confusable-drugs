Plain-language explanation

One combined Filipino-aware check

It creates one comparison-only reading form using selected consonant and vowel substitutions, then measures similarity. It does not split people into Tagalog and Bisaya groups, and the published medicine names are never changed.

1

Normalize for comparison

Capitalization, spaces and punctuation are simplified.

2

Combine consonant readings

ph/p, th/t, sh/s, f/p, v/b, and z/s.

3

Combine vowel readings

e/i and o/u are compared as possible reading substitutions for all Filipino-aware screening.

4

Score, then review

The combined Jaro–Winkler score is only one signal among several.
